History

Origins (1940s)

Mountain Dew was created in 1940 by Tennessee beverage bottlers Barney and Ally Hartman in Knoxville, Tennessee. The brothers originally intended it as a mixer for whiskey — the name "Mountain Dew" is itself an Appalachian slang term for moonshine. The original formula was a lemon-lime soda with a yellow-green hue, intended to complement the local moonshine tradition.

In 1961, Bill Bridgforth purchased the rights and reformulated the drink. That same year, Tip Corporation of Marion, Virginia acquired the brand and produced a revised version with a hillbilly-themed marketing campaign, featuring the cartoon character Willy the Hillbilly on its label.

Pepsi Acquisition (1964)

PepsiCo purchased Mountain Dew from Tip Corporation in 1964. Under Pepsi's ownership, the brand was reformulated and aggressively marketed. Pepsi pushed Mountain Dew as an energetic, youth-oriented soft drink, a positioning that would define it for decades.

Early Pepsi-era Mountain Dew was marketed with the slogan "Yahoo Mountain Dew! It'll tickle yore innards!" — leaning into the rustic, Southern/Appalachian identity. The original glass bottle featured a cartoon hillbilly shooting at an outhouse, a far cry from the extreme-sports branding that came later.

National Expansion (1969–1990s)

Mountain Dew was rolled out nationally by PepsiCo in 1969. During the 1970s and 1980s, advertising shifted away from the hillbilly theme toward outdoor adventure and action sports. By the late 1980s, Mountain Dew was consistently ranked among the top-selling soft drinks in the United States.

The 1990s saw Mountain Dew rebranded as the go-to drink for extreme sports. The iconic "Do the Dew" campaign launched in 1992, featuring skateboarding, snowboarding, BMX, and other adrenaline-fueled activities. This campaign resonated strongly with Gen X and younger consumers.

Diet Mountain Dew was introduced in 1988, broadening the brand's consumer base significantly.

Modern Era (2000s–Present)

The 2000s ushered in an explosion of new Mountain Dew flavors. The brand introduced its first major variant, Mountain Dew Code Red, in 2001, which became enormously popular and demonstrated consumer appetite for new Dew flavors. This triggered a multi-decade strategy of constantly launching new flavors, limited editions, and fan-driven "DEWcision" campaigns.

The DEWmocracy campaigns (2007–2012) allowed fans to vote on new permanent flavors, resulting in flavors like Voltage, White Out, and Typhoon. MTN DEW became the official stylized branding in 2009 when PepsiCo redesigned the logo.

In the 2010s and 2020s, Mountain Dew expanded into energy drinks (Game Fuel, Kickstart, Rise Energy), partnered heavily with video game culture and esports, and continued to release dozens of new permanent and limited flavors globally. By the early 2020s, Mountain Dew was the 4th best-selling carbonated soft drink in the United States.
